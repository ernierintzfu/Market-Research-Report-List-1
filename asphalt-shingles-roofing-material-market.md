<p><p>Asphalt shingles roofing materials are primarily classified into two types: organic and fiberglass. Organic asphalt shingles contain a base of cellulose fibers, often recycled paper, providing flexibility and a heavier weight. They are known for their durability but can be more susceptible to moisture. In contrast, fiberglass asphalt shingles utilize a fiberglass mat for reinforcement, making them lighter and more resistant to fire and moisture. Both types offer a variety of styles and colors, catering to diverse aesthetic preferences and climate conditions.</p></p>
