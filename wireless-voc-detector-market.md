<p><ul><li>PID</li><li>Metal-oxide Semiconductor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wireless VOC (volatile organic compound) detector market primarily features two types of technologies: Photoionization Detectors (PID) and Metal-Oxide Semiconductors (MOS). PID sensors utilize ultraviolet light to ionize gas molecules, providing accurate detection of low concentrations of VOCs, making them suitable for industrial applications. In contrast, MOS sensors operate by measuring changes in electrical resistance caused by gas interactions, offering cost-effective solutions for indoor air quality monitoring. Both types play vital roles in environmental safety and indoor air management.</p></p>
