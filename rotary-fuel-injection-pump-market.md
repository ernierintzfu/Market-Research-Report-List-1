<p><p>The Rotary Fuel Injection Pump market is segmented into low pressure and high pressure systems. Low pressure pumps typically operate at lower fuel delivery rates, suitable for applications requiring less precise fuel atomization, often found in older engine models. In contrast, high pressure pumps generate greater fuel pressure, essential for modern diesel engines that demand efficient atomization for optimal combustion and performance. The choice between these pump types depends on engine design, performance requirements, and emission standards.</p></p>
