<p><p>Rail market types can be categorized mainly into heavy rail and light rail systems. Heavy rail typically refers to urban transit systems designed for high passenger volumes, operating on dedicated tracks, often featuring longer trains, higher speeds, and greater capacity. Light rail, in contrast, is generally designed for shorter trips, often sharing roadways with other vehicles and featuring lighter-weight vehicles. Both systems serve crucial roles in urban transportation, improving mobility and reducing congestion in densely populated areas.</p></p>
